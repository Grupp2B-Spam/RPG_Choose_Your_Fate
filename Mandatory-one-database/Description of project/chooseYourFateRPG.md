* Chapters

	Chapters: There are multiple chapters split into sections that have a main theme. 

	Multiple phases of the game in which we name (a choice can change the main theme/ section of the next chapters. 

* Scenes

	Scenes: Each scene consists of one or multiple choices in which your choice heavily effects which direction your story will go in. 

	Scenes are area based lets say you are in a "courtyard" of a castle that whole area or the multiple choices there is one scene. 

	However if you move past the "countyard" and go inside into the "grand hall" this will now be a new scene.

* Choices

	Choices: Each choice decides your next set of choices. 

	The player gets 1-5 choices per scene. Some choices lock you out from other choices, you can't explore them all within one playthrough/ game. 

	The idea of a choice is to take one that you believe is right and believe will bring you to the correct path in which you want to play.

* Consequences

	Consequences: The consequences of your choices could lead you to be locked out of other/ the rest of the choices. 

	Some choices can lead you to fights in which you might lose or win (within fights it's its own scene) depending on this win or loss you will have further choices to be made and locked out of other further choices.

* Endings

	Endings: An ending can be three types of things. 

	Ending of a chapter usually means moving to a new area of the game (ex. moving from the castle to travelling(?) to a battlefield). 

	Ending of a scene means moving to a new location within the chapter and this would mean you get to see your new set of choice(s). 

	Ending of a "path" usually means that you either died or have reached the end of the story we have written so far.

* Progress

	Progress: Progress is measured per chapter and within the chapter it is measured per scene. 

	So progress could look like Chp4Scn3 we can't put a max number on chapters because depending on the player's choices more or less chapters will show up during the playthrough.
